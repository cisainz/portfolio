"Group Climate and Process"- Blog Post

01/30/20

This past week we talked about some fundamentals for working on a project with a group. Cheryl used Pixar as an example, which I thought was a good choice seeing that it is possibly the most collaborative companies in the world.With the video pixar we saw how the environment where teams work effect communication.

What we mostly talked about this week is the fundamentals on how to be a good communicator on your team and what good communication can do for a team.

We went over what is a healthy group climate:

  • Openness - Team members should never feel like they have to hold back their ideas and opinions. Memebers need to be transparent and feel free to ask questions in order to be effective and efficient.

  • Sef-Disclosure - Members building personal connections with each other and no only talking about "business" helps builds bonds, and allows them to trust one another.

  • Support - Members always being present in conversation and always lending a helping hand when available.

  • Respect - Members must recognize that each person on the roster has their own experience and skills. Members must being open to learning from each other and letting each person do their part without micro-managing them.

Along with effective communication and how to build a healthy climate, we also discussed the Group Process:

  • Accountabilty - Members must be responsible for their actions and productivity. They must be sure to do their part for the project.

  • Constructive Feedback - Members should feel free to give constructive criticism when it is warrented. Be sure to add some positive re-enforcement with your crtique. Point out bothe goods and the bads.

  • Problem Solve - Makes sure to identify problems and include everyone on finding/deciding a solution.

  • Knowledge of Roles - It is important to collaborate and help other members out, but be sure that you are simply providing support and not just taking over. Allow everyone to do their part.



"Marshmallow Tower" - Blog Post

03/03/20

I think the key to this project was preparation.The whole team came together and mentally mapped out how we would build our tower and that gave us the blueprints for the structure. This way we didn't waste any time when the timer started and we had to build within that time frame.

Since we did do that preparation, it was all hands on deck and everyone could work simultaneously to build the structure. Because of that we were able to complete the tower long before others did and sit there, admittedly, with a mountain of arrogance as we watched the other teams finish up.


"Constructive Criticism" - Blog Post

02/25/20

My partner's project was about the Ford Mustang car, which I would personally say they executed expertly. I was very impressed with their project. Honestly I didn't have any real notes other than about fonts to use.

In all honesty, I don't feel that the sandwich form was very helpful to me personally. I have personally gained so much experience with critiquing people's work through my many art classes through the years. That isn't to say that form isn't objectively not useful, but for as an individual it wasn't really necessary.

As far as my preference of the two roles during the critique, I'm indifferent. I feel I'm really good at evaluating others work without them seeing it as a personal attack. I think I'm good about being encouraging and providing solutions to problems. I also think I'm good at being critique. I never feel like they are just trying to tear me down. I know that they are simply just giving me their opinion and perspective to so I can make my project the best it can be.
